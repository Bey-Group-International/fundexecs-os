# BGI / Fund I / FundExecs Technologies — Relationship Memo

*Confidential · July 2026*

> Purpose: explain, plainly and completely, how Bey Group International (BGI), BGI Fund I, and FundExecs Technologies relate — commercially, legally, and in governance — so that an investor in any one of the three understands exactly what they are and are not buying. Final structure is subject to counsel; this memo states the intended and operating arrangement.

---

**The one-sentence version:** BGI is the founder's investment platform; BGI Fund I is the fund that platform sponsors; FundExecs Technologies is a separate software company whose product, FundExecs OS, BGI and Fund I use as their operating system — and each has its own investors, its own instrument, and its own risk.

## 2. Why the three exist together

FundExecs OS was born from the founder's operating pain inside BGI's advisory work: 4+ years serving PE funds and family offices, three hours a day lost to information movement. BGI's capital-formation program (including The $100M Room salon methodology) became the **first live workload**: the Capital Raiser, Curator, Rainmaker, and Executive Advisor agents encode playbooks proven in that program, and BGI Fund I's raise runs on the platform today.

This is deliberate strategy, not entanglement:

- **For FundExecs:** a demanding, real, in-house design partner from day one — live workflows, honest feedback, credible case study, zero-CAC first customer.
- **For BGI/Fund I:** operating leverage — an emerging platform runs with institutional-grade process, records, and audit trails from inception ("the Moorish OS" in BGI's fund thesis refers to this operating discipline, delivered through FundExecs OS).
- **For investors in either:** the relationship is a feature *only if* it is governed at arm's length — hence the rest of this memo.

## 3. Capital separation — what each investor owns

This is the paragraph that matters most:

1. **Seed investors in FundExecs Technologies** own equity (or SAFEs) in the software company only. They have **no interest** in BGI, the Founding Capital Circle, or BGI Fund I, and no exposure to fund performance except indirectly as Fund I is a customer.
2. **Founding Capital Circle participants** hold a contractual participation in defined **BGI management-company revenue** (15% pool, 2x cap, triggered at Fund I first close, per its own documentation). They own **no part of FundExecs Technologies** and no LP interest in Fund I.
3. **Fund I LPs** own limited-partner interests in the fund, per its LPA. They own no part of BGI's management company or FundExecs Technologies.

No instrument converts into, or carries rights over, either of the other two entities. Marketing materials for each raise state this separation explicitly.

## 4. Intellectual property

- **FundExecs OS — code, schema, agent architecture, and brand — is owned by FundExecs Technologies.** All contributions are made under IP assignment; the repository and its history are the company's asset.
- **Domain playbooks:** capital-formation methodology developed within BGI (e.g., the $100M Room salon structure, capital-raiser playbooks) is licensed or assigned into FundExecs' Brain knowledge bases under a written agreement (counsel; formalized at or before seed close), so the software company's agents stand on clean IP.
- Any founder IP created before incorporation is captured by a standard founder IP assignment at incorporation/financing.

## 5. Commercial terms between the entities

- **BGI / Fund I as customer:** paid subscription on standard published terms (Scale tier as repriced), with any design-partner discount documented and time-bound — the same offer available to external design partners. No perpetual free use; no revenue-share back to BGI.
- **No cross-guarantees, no commingling:** separate bank accounts, books, and counsel. FundExecs seed capital is not used to fund BGI/Fund I activities, and Founding Capital Circle proceeds are not used to fund FundExecs (each raise's use-of-funds is its own).
- **Case-study and demo rights:** FundExecs may reference Fund I workflows in anonymized/approved form; Fund I controls its LP-facing confidentiality.

## 6. Governance of the conflict

The founder controls all three entities today. Measures that keep the relationship clean:

1. **Disclosure first.** This memo sits in all three data rooms; every investor sees the same picture.
2. **Arm's-length pricing** for the vendor relationship (§5), benchmarked against external design-partner terms.
3. **Time allocation:** post-seed, the founder's operating commitment is to FundExecs Technologies; BGI/Fund I responsibilities are disclosed to seed investors, with fund-side operating load carried by fund-side hires/partners as Fund I scales. (Investors should diligence this directly with the founder.)
4. **Board/observer rights** granted with the seed round give FundExecs investors visibility into any related-party transaction; related-party dealings above a de-minimis threshold require board approval.
5. **Data isolation by architecture:** BGI's org on FundExecs OS is subject to the same row-level-security tenancy as any customer — the software company's staff and other customers have no privileged access to Fund I's LP data, and vice versa.

## 8. Risks of the relationship (stated plainly)

|                      Risk                       |                                                         Mitigation                                                          |
|-------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Founder bandwidth split across three entities   | §6.3 commitment + seed-funded team; diligence directly                                                                      |
| Optics of self-dealing (vendor terms, IP)       | Written arm's-length agreements, disclosure, board approval of related-party transactions                                   |
| Confusion between the raises                    | Capital separation language (§3) in every instrument's materials; different instruments, different banks, different counsel |
| Fund I underperformance reflecting on FundExecs | FundExecs' customer base diversifies beyond BGI in year one (document 12); Fund I is a customer, not collateral             |
| Brain/playbook IP provenance                    | License/assignment executed at or before seed close (§4)                                                                    |

## 9. Bottom line

BGI and Fund I are FundExecs Technologies' origin story and first customer — not its parent, its guarantor, or its risk. The three entities are kept separate in capital, books, IP, and governance, and connected only by a founder, a vendor agreement at arm's length, and a shared conviction: **the next generation of investment firms will be operators plus an AI operating system.** BGI Fund I is simply the first firm to run that way.
