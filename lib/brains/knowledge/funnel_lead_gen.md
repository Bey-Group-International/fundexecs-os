## CAPTCHA / Anti-Spam

Best for:

- Protecting forms
- Preventing bots
- Reducing fake submissions
- Protecting event forms
- Protecting investor inquiry forms

Use CAPTCHA when:

- Forms are public.
- Paid ads are driving traffic.
- Spam submissions increase.
- High-value investor forms are exposed.
- Business submission forms receive junk.

Recommended options:

- Google reCAPTCHA
- Cloudflare Turnstile
- hCaptcha

Best practices:

- Use invisible or low-friction CAPTCHA.
- Do not make qualified investors fight annoying verification.
- Add honeypot fields when possible.
- Use email verification for sensitive workflows.
- Use manual review for LP and investment-related submissions.

# 17. Compliance and Risk Notes

The funnel brain must protect BGI from careless language.

Do not:

- Promise returns.
- Say investments are guaranteed.
- Imply public solicitation if materials are private.
- Collect investment capital through Square or Google Pay without legal approval.
- Share CIMs publicly without access control.
- Send sensitive materials without tracking or approval.
- Use misleading testimonials.
- Overstate capital commitments.
- Use fake scarcity.
- Call someone an investor if they have not committed.

Use safer language:

- “Request access”
- “Explore fit”
- “Review materials”
- “Strategic conversation”
- “Qualified investors”
- “Subject to eligibility”
- “For informational purposes”
- “No offer to sell securities is made through this page”

Recommended disclaimer:

> This page is for informational purposes only and does not constitute an offer to sell or a solicitation of an offer to buy securities. Any investment opportunity, if applicable, will be made only through appropriate offering documents and to eligible investors.
