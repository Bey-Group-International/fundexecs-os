## 7. Prospect Scoring System

Score each prospect from 1 to 10.

### Investor Prospect Score

Give points for:

- Has capital or influence over capital: +2
- Invests in private markets: +2
- Has family office / allocator / UHNW access: +2
- Has interest in diverse, emerging, faith-aligned, or impact managers: +1
- Has dealmaking history: +1
- Has strategic network value: +1
- Has clear reason BGI fits: +1

**Score interpretation:**

- 9–10: Priority target. Pursue immediately.
- 7–8: Strong prospect. Warm outreach.
- 5–6: Useful relationship. Nurture.
- 1–4: Low fit unless they provide access.

### Company Acquisition Score

Give points for:

- Revenue $1M–$20M+: +2
- Positive EBITDA or cash flow: +2
- Founder-led / succession issue: +1
- Existing management team: +1
- Fragmented industry: +1
- Repeat customers or recurring revenue: +1
- Operational improvement opportunity: +1
- Strategic fit with BGI verticals: +1

**Score interpretation:**

- 9–10: Immediate acquisition review.
- 7–8: Strong target. Request details.
- 5–6: Watchlist / relationship build.
- 1–4: Pass unless strategic.

## 11. Qualification Framework

Every prospect should be qualified through the following questions.

### Investor Qualification

Ask:

1. Do they currently allocate to private equity, direct deals, or alternative investments?
2. What check size is realistic?
3. Are they investing personally, through a family office, through a fund, or through a network?
4. Are they looking for fund exposure, direct deals, co-investments, or strategic partnerships?
5. What sectors do they avoid?
6. What timeline are they operating on?
7. Who else is involved in approval?
8. What materials do they need before a second conversation?
9. Are they a decision-maker, influencer, or connector?
10. What would make BGI a strong fit for their mandate?

### Business Owner Qualification

Ask:

1. What is annual revenue?
2. What is EBITDA or seller discretionary earnings?
3. Why is the owner exploring a transition?
4. Is the owner looking for full exit, partial exit, growth partner, or recapitalization?
5. Is there a management team in place?
6. How concentrated is revenue?
7. What are the biggest operational bottlenecks?
8. What would the owner want protected after a transaction?
9. Is there debt, litigation, tax exposure, or deferred maintenance?
10. What valuation range or structure would they consider?

### Connector Qualification

Ask:

1. Who exactly can they introduce BGI to?
2. Are those relationships warm and trusted?
3. Do they know investors, business owners, lenders, or operators?
4. What would make the relationship valuable to them?
5. Are they seeking referral economics, visibility, strategic access, or partnership?
6. Can they bring qualified people to a private event?
7. Can they make 3–5 immediate introductions?
8. Do they understand BGI’s criteria?
9. Are they serious or just networking?
10. What next action can they take within 7 days?

## 12. Call Structure

### Opening

> I appreciate the time. The purpose of this call is simple: determine whether there is a real strategic fit. If there is, we can define next steps. If not, we will be direct and preserve the relationship.

### Frame

> BGI is building a control-oriented lower-middle-market investment platform. We are focused on cash-flowing or EBITDA-positive businesses where better governance, operating discipline, technology, and strategic capital can unlock value.

### Discovery

Ask questions based on the prospect type. Listen for urgency, capital, authority, need, timing, and fit.

### Positioning

Tie BGI directly to their problem.

> Based on what you shared, the fit may be around three things: access, structure, and execution. You have interest in private ownership, but the missing piece appears to be a platform with disciplined sourcing and operating capability. That is exactly where BGI is positioned.

### Close

Do not end with vague follow-up.

Use:

> The clean next step is to get you the right materials and schedule a focused follow-up around fit, structure, and allocation.

Or:

> The right next move is for you to introduce us to the two or three people in your network who are already thinking about private ownership, direct deals, or family office allocation.

Or:

> If the company fits our acquisition criteria, the next step is for us to review basic financials under confidentiality and determine whether a structure makes sense.

## 15. Objection Handling

### Objection: “We are not LPs.”

Response:

> Understood. The better question may be whether you sit near people who are — family offices, operators, lenders, founders, or strategic capital. If so, this may still be relevant as a relationship and deal-flow conversation rather than an LP conversation.

---

### Objection: “Send me more information.”

Response:

> I can send the overview. To make sure I send the right materials, are you looking at this as a potential investor, connector, operator, lender, or acquisition relationship?

---

### Objection: “We do not invest in first-time funds.”

Response:

> Understood. Some groups have that policy. Where this may still fit is through co-investment, direct deal review, strategic introductions, or tracking the platform as BGI builds the first portfolio. Would that be more relevant?

---

### Objection: “What is your track record?”

Response:

> Fair question. BGI is being built from an advisory, sourcing, operating, and relationship track record rather than a conventional institutional fund history. The edge is in deal access, operating discipline, cultural positioning, and the ability to organize capital around overlooked lower-middle-market opportunities. For investors who require a long realized fund history, this may not be the fit today. For investors who understand emerging manager upside and platform formation, the timing is exactly why the access is valuable.

---

### Objection: “We are focused on larger managers.”

Response:

> That makes sense. Larger managers provide institutional comfort, but they rarely offer early access, co-investment proximity, or differentiated sourcing. BGI is not trying to compete with mega-funds. We are building in the part of the market where control, relationships, and operational improvement still matter.

---

### Objection: “Circle back later.”

Response:

> We can do that, but I want to be direct. If this is not relevant, I would rather know now. If it is relevant but timing is the issue, the right move is to define what would need to be true for this to become actionable.

---

### Objection: “We only make direct investments.”

Response:

> That may actually be a strong fit. BGI can be relevant as a sponsor, sourcing partner, operator-led platform, or co-investment channel. The question is whether your direct mandate aligns with lower-middle-market control opportunities.

---

### Objection: “We are not raising or selling.”

Response to business owner:

> Understood. We are not assuming you are. We build relationships before transition moments occur. If ownership, succession, growth, or liquidity becomes relevant, it is better to already know serious buyers and strategic partners before pressure enters the equation.
