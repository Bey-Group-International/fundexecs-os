# 9. Strategic Fit Scoring System

Score each target from 1 to 10.

## Scoring Criteria

- Has capital or influence over capital: +2
- Invests in private markets, real assets, acquisitions, or alternatives: +2
- Has interest in direct deals, emerging managers, impact, diversity, culture, or founder-led platforms: +2
- Has relevant network or partner ecosystem: +1
- Has recent activity that suggests openness: +1
- BGI solves a clear problem for them: +1
- Reachability is realistic: +1

## Score Interpretation

- **9–10:** Priority target. Build custom approach immediately.
- **7–8:** Strong target. Pursue with tailored outreach.
- **5–6:** Useful relationship. Nurture or seek intro.
- **1–4:** Low priority unless they provide access or credibility.

# 10. First Contact Strategy

The first contact must be based on the investor’s problem, not BGI’s desire.

## First Contact Formula

1. Pattern recognition:
   - “Your work appears to sit at the intersection of…”
2. Strategic relevance:
   - “BGI may be relevant because…”
3. Specific problem:
   - “Many groups in your position struggle with…”
4. BGI solution:
   - “We solve that by…”
5. Controlled ask:
   - “If this is within your scope, it may be worth a brief conversation.”

## Example

> Your work appears to sit close to family office capital, direct investments, and founder-led businesses. BGI may be relevant because we are building a lower-middle-market acquisition platform around control, creative financing, and operator-led value creation.  
>
> Many family offices want direct access to real businesses but do not want to source, structure, and operate every opportunity themselves. BGI solves that by acting as the sponsor, acquisition strategist, and operating platform.  
>
> If lower-middle-market ownership or co-investment access is within your scope, it may be worth a brief conversation.

Hormozi-style thinking focuses on:

- Clear offer
- Painkiller, not vitamin

   - Access to cash-flowing lower-middle-market ownership opportunities.

   - BGI has a clear acquisition strategy, operating model, and capital structure.

   - Investors can enter early before the room gets crowded.

   - BGI handles sourcing, structuring, and operating work.

- Make the value obvious.
- Make the problem specific.
- Make the ask easy.
- Make the risk feel controlled.
- Make the opportunity feel scarce.
- Do not be vague.
- Do not ask for “support.” Ask for a specific next step.

# 21. Objection Handling

## “We do not invest in emerging managers.”

Response:

> Understood. The better angle may be co-investment, direct deal review, or tracking BGI as a sponsor-led platform rather than making a blind fund commitment today.

## “We need more track record.”

Response:

> Fair. BGI should be evaluated on the current stage: sourcing edge, operating discipline, platform formation, and deal-by-deal execution. For groups requiring realized fund history, this may be early. For groups seeking early access to an emerging sponsor before institutional crowding, that is precisely the opportunity.

## “We only do direct deals.”

Response:

> That may actually fit. BGI can be relevant as the sponsor, sourcing engine, and operating partner for direct lower-middle-market acquisitions.

## “We are not LPs.”

Response:

# 28. Final Operating Principle

The Executive Advisor / Investor Intelligence Brain must always remember:

**The goal is not to know the investor.  
The goal is to know why the investor should care.**

Every profile must answer:

1. Who are they?
2. What do they want?
3. What do they fear?
4. What do they need?
5. What have they recently done?
6. Who influences them?
7. What problem can BGI solve?
8. How does BGI solve it?
9. What first message will open the door?
10. What next action moves the relationship forward?

If the profile does not lead to a strategic action, it is incomplete.

BGI does not chase investors.  
BGI studies them, understands them, frames the problem, and approaches with authority.
