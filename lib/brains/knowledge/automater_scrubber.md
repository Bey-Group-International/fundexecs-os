# 4. Core Scrubbing Philosophy

Scrubbing means separating signal from noise.

The brain must identify:

## High-Value Signals

- Investor asks for materials
- Family office responds
- Capital provider requests call
- Business owner shares revenue/EBITDA
- Broker sends deal
- Seller shows motivation
- Connector offers intros
- Operator asks about ownership
- Lender asks for financing memo
- Papermark shows repeated document views
- Calendly call is booked
- Event VIP registers
- Media opportunity appears
- Legal document is attached
- Counterparty sends terms
- Warm introduction is made

## Medium-Value Signals

- General interest
- Request for more info
- Social engagement from relevant person
- Event registration from unknown person
- Business inquiry without financial details
- Connector with vague network
- Operator without track record
- Lender asking for more context

## Low-Value Signals

- Spam
- Generic vendor pitch
- Unqualified investor
- No capital/no access/no deal
- Bad-fit business
- Purely promotional message
- No response after full cadence
- Irrelevant social comment

The brain should not treat every message equally.  
It should protect leadership attention.

# 9. Automation Rules

## Rule 1: Investor Asks for Materials

Trigger:

- Email contains: deck, materials, overview, investor summary, send info, learn more, fund details

Action:

1. Tag as Investor Lead.
2. Score lead.
3. Route to Executive Advisor Brain for profile.
4. Route to Rainmaker Brain for reply.
5. Route to Legal/Admin Brain if securities-sensitive.
6. Send Papermark link if approved.
7. Schedule follow-up in 2 days.
8. Move to Materials Sent stage.

---

## Rule 2: Family Office or Allocator Responds

Trigger:

- Sender domain or language indicates family office, allocator, investment office, CIO, principal, partner, managing director.

Action:

1. Priority escalation.
2. Route to Executive Advisor Brain.
3. Draft custom response.
4. Notify leadership.
5. Schedule investor fit call.
6. Prepare meeting brief.

---

## Rule 3: Broker Sends Deal

Trigger:

- Email contains: teaser, CIM, business for sale, acquisition opportunity, EBITDA, revenue, asking price, NDA.

Action:

1. Tag as Deal / Broker.
2. Extract business details.
3. Route to Deal Sourcer Brain.
4. If NDA attached, route Legal/Admin.
5. Create deal record.
6. Score deal.
7. Draft broker response.
8. Request missing info.

---

## Rule 4: Business Owner Submits Company

Trigger:

- Form or email includes business name, revenue, transition, sale, succession, partnership.

Action:

1. Tag as Business Submission.
2. Route to Deal Sourcer.
3. Create deal pipeline record.
4. If qualified, send Calendly acquisition review.
5. If not enough info, request financial snapshot.
6. Notify leadership if revenue/EBITDA is strong.

---

## Rule 5: Connector Offers Introductions

Trigger:

- Email contains: introduce you, know investors, family office, capital, lenders, partners, network.

Action:

1. Tag as Capital Connector.
2. Extract potential intro types.
3. Score connector.
4. Route to Rainmaker.
5. Send connector application or alignment call link.
6. Ask for 3 specific introductions.

---

## Rule 6: Operator Wants to Help

Trigger:

- Email/social contains: operator, CEO, GM, run business, equity, ownership, operating partner, acquisition support.

Action:

1. Tag as Operator.
2. Extract sector and experience.
3. Route to Executive Advisor / Deal Sourcer.
4. Send operator application.
5. Schedule operator fit call if strong.

---

## Rule 7: Lender or Capital Provider Responds

Trigger:

- Email contains: financing, loan, private credit, debt, SBA, ABL, bridge, term sheet, credit box.

Action:

1. Tag as Lender / Capital Provider.
2. Route to Capital Connector Brain.
3. Extract credit box info.
4. Send financing overview if appropriate.
5. Schedule financing discussion.
6. Log in financing pipeline.

---

## Rule 8: Papermark Document Viewed

Trigger:

- Papermark notification.

Action:

1. Identify viewer.
2. Update CRM.
3. If multiple views or long engagement, tag Hot.
4. Draft follow-up.
5. Move to Materials Engaged.
6. Schedule call ask.

---

## Rule 9: Calendly Call Booked

Trigger:

- Calendly notification.

Action:

1. Update CRM stage to Call Booked.
2. Notify Slack.
3. Route to Executive Advisor for meeting brief.
4. Route to Rainmaker for call script.
5. Send confirmation and prep material.
6. Schedule post-call follow-up task.

---

## Rule 10: Luma Event Registration

Trigger:

- Luma registration.

Action:

1. Add attendee to Event Pipeline.
2. Tag by attendee type.
3. Send confirmation.
4. Add to event reminder sequence.
5. If VIP, notify leadership.
6. After event, route based on attendee type.

---

## Rule 11: Legal Document Attached

Trigger:

- Attachment contains NDA, LOI, agreement, term sheet, subscription, operating agreement, vendor contract.

Action:

1. Tag Legal Review.
2. Route to Legal/Admin Brain.
3. Do not sign or approve automatically.
4. Extract parties, document type, deadlines.
5. Prepare issue summary.
6. Escalate if material.

# 13. Standard Outreach Sequences

## Investor Sequence

### Step 1: First Contact

> I am reaching out because your work appears aligned with private market access, direct investments, or differentiated sponsor relationships. BGI is building a control-oriented lower-middle-market acquisition platform around creative financing, operator-led execution, and culture-driven governance. If this sits within your mandate, it may be worth a brief conversation.

### Step 2: Follow-Up

> Bringing this back to the top. The most relevant angle may be lower-middle-market ownership access and co-investment-style exposure, not a generic fund pitch. Worth a short fit conversation?

### Step 3: Final Follow-Up

> I do not want to crowd your inbox. Should I treat this as outside your scope, or is there someone better suited for private markets, direct deals, or family office allocation?

---

## Capital Connector Sequence

### Step 1

> BGI is aligning serious connectors who can route qualified capital, lenders, business owners, operators, or strategic partners into the ecosystem. This is not about random introductions. It is about putting the right people in the right room.

### Step 2

> The strongest connector relationships are specific. Who can you introduce: investors, lenders, owners, operators, or strategic partners?

### Step 3

> If you can make 3 qualified introductions in the next 30 days, it is worth scheduling an alignment call.

---

## Business Owner Sequence

### Step 1

> I came across your company and believe there may be strategic fit with Bey Group International. BGI reviews founder-owned businesses where succession, liquidity, growth, or transition may require a disciplined acquisition partner.

### Step 2

> We are not approaching this casually. If the business has real revenue, cash flow, and the right transition motivation, BGI may be a serious buyer or strategic partner.

### Step 3

> The clean next step is a short acquisition review or a basic financial snapshot to determine fit.

---

## Operator Sequence

### Step 1

> Your operating background may be relevant to BGI’s acquisition platform. We are aligning operators who can help lead, improve, or scale lower-middle-market businesses post-acquisition.

### Step 2

> Strong operators should not always remain employees or consultants. Where there is fit, BGI can pair operating ability with capital strategy and ownership upside.

### Step 3

> Worth a short operator fit conversation?

# 23. Quality Control Rules

Before sending or automating anything, check:

## Messaging

- Is it clear?
- Is it personal enough?
- Is the CTA specific?
- Does it sound institutional?
- Does it avoid desperation?
- Does it avoid unsupported claims?

## Compliance

- Does it mention investment returns?
- Does it imply guaranteed outcomes?
- Does it solicit investment improperly?
- Does it share private materials publicly?
- Does it need legal review?

## Data

- Is contact info correct?
- Is the lead duplicate?
- Is the pipeline stage correct?
- Is the follow-up date set?
- Are tags applied?

## Production

- Is the asset approved?
- Is the correct link attached?
- Is tracking enabled?
- Is the post scheduled correctly?
- Is the CTA working?

# 24. Compliance Guardrails

The Automater / Scrubber must not:

- Send investment subscription instructions without approval.
- Promise returns.
- Say an investor is accepted before qualification.
- Auto-send confidential CIMs publicly.
- Auto-sign or approve legal documents.
- Pay or promise finder fees without legal review.
- Use aggressive spam tactics.
- Scrape or contact people in ways that violate platform rules or privacy laws.
- Use misleading subject lines.
- Misrepresent BGI’s capital, commitments, or track record.
- Share personal data without proper purpose.

Safe language:

- “Request investor access”
- “Explore fit”
- “Private review”
- “Subject to eligibility”
- “For informational purposes”
- “Schedule a fit conversation”
- “Submit for review”
- “Qualified next step”
