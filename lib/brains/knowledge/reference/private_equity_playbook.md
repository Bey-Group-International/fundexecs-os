# Private Equity Playbook — Reference Cheat Sheet

> Shared reference knowledge for the deal, capital, and advisory Brains.
> A compact operator's field guide to how private equity investing works end to
> end: the skills, the deal process, valuation methods, terms, capital
> structure, LBO mechanics, metrics, diligence, and value creation.
> Invest. Build. Grow. Exit.

---

## 1. Key Skills

- **Financial Modeling** — Build LBO, DCF, and operating models.
- **Valuation** — Assess value and deal price.
- **Accounting** — Master the financial statements.
- **Due Diligence** — Identify risks and opportunities.
- **Deal Sourcing** — Find and evaluate opportunities.
- **Negotiation** — Structure win–win transactions.
- **Portfolio Management** — Create value and drive performance.

---

## 2. Private Equity Deal Process

1. **Source** — Generate investment opportunities.
2. **Screen** — Evaluate strategic fit, industry, and financials.
3. **Due Diligence** — Deep dive on business, risks, and management.
4. **Value** — Build valuation and LBO models.
5. **Execute** — Negotiate terms and close the deal.
6. **Operate** — Improve performance and create value.
7. **Exit** — Realize value via sale or IPO.

---

## 3. Valuation Methods

- **Comparable Company Analysis** — Value based on public company multiples.
- **Precedent Transactions** — Value based on similar acquisition transactions.
- **DCF Analysis** — Value based on future cash flows.
- **LBO Analysis** — Evaluate returns under different scenarios.
- **Asset-Based Valuation** — Value based on net asset value.

## 5. Capital Structure

From lowest risk / lowest return to highest risk / highest return:

- **Senior Debt** — Lowest risk, fixed interest.
- **Mezzanine Debt** — Subordinated debt, higher return.
- **Preferred Equity** — Hybrid security, priority over common.
- **Common Equity** — Highest risk, highest return.

---

## 6. LBO Model Basics

**Sources of capital**
- Equity Contribution
- Senior Debt
- Mezzanine Financing

**Uses of capital**
- Purchase Equity
- Refinance Debt
- Transaction Fees

**Key drivers of returns**
- Revenue Growth
- EBITDA Margin
- Leverage (Deleveraging)
- Exit Multiple
- Holding Period

**Target returns**
- IRR: 20%+
- MOIC: 2x – 3x+

---

## 7. Common PE Metrics

|     Metric     |         Formula          |
|----------------|--------------------------|
| EBITDA Margin  | EBITDA / Revenue         |
| Net Debt       | Total Debt − Cash        |
| Debt / EBITDA  | Total Debt / EBITDA      |
| ROIC           | NOPAT / Invested Capital |
| Free Cash Flow | CFO − Capex              |
| Exit Value     | EBITDA × Exit Multiple   |
| Equity Value   | Exit Value − Net Debt    |

## 9. Due Diligence Checklist

- Review financial statements.
- Analyze customer concentration.
- Evaluate management team.
- Assess market and competition.
- Review contracts and legal issues.
- Verify quality of earnings.
- Assess operational efficiency.
- Identify key risks and mitigants.

---

## 10. Value Creation Levers

- **Revenue Growth** — Organic growth and pricing power.
- **Margin Expansion** — Cost optimization and efficiency.
- **Strategic Initiatives** — Add-on acquisitions, new markets.
- **Capital Structure** — Optimize leverage and cost of capital.
- **Talent & Culture** — Hire, retain, and build strong teams.
- **Operational Excellence** — Processes, systems, technology.

---

## 11. Key Takeaways

- **Buy right.** Invest in high-quality businesses at the right price.
- **Build value.** Improve operations, grow revenue, and expand margins.
- **Focus on cash flow.** Strong cash flow drives returns.
- **Plan the exit.** A great exit creates exceptional returns.
- **Alignment wins.** Align interests with management and investors.
