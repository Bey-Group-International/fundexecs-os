# 5. Moorish Culture Design Influence

The brand can draw from Moorish culture through:

- Geometry
- Symmetry
- Arches
- Lattice patterns
- Tile-inspired borders
- Architectural lines
- Desert, stone, ivory, gold, deep green, black, clay, sand, and bronze tones
- Historic manuscript feel
- Calligraphic influence used sparingly
- Star motifs used subtly
- Compass and navigation symbolism
- Doorways, gateways, and courtyards
- Trade routes and merchant-house themes
- Sovereign seals and institutional crests
- Minimal ornamental dividers
- Old-world scholarship paired with modern finance

The brand should avoid:

- Overuse of patterns
- Loud colors
- Clip-art Islamic or Moorish symbols
- Heavy religious imagery
- Decorative borders on every page
- Excessive gold
- Excessive flags or seals
- Anything that makes the material feel like a cultural pamphlet instead of an investment document

Design principle:

> Moorish influence should appear as quiet authority, not visual noise.

- Use one serif and one sans-serif.

- Use generous spacing.
- Use short headlines.
- Use strong section hierarchy.

# 9. Design Rules

## Minimalist Executive Design Rules

1. One idea per slide or page.
2. More whitespace than decoration.
3. Use hierarchy, not clutter.
4. Use strong titles that make a point.
5. Use short body copy.
6. Use visual dividers sparingly.
7. Use charts only when they clarify.
8. Use icons rarely and consistently.
9. Use Moorish patterns only as accents.
10. Use gold as punctuation, not paint.

## Page Layout Rules

- Large title
- Short subtitle
- Clear body blocks
- Minimal footer
- Optional thin accent line
- One key visual per page
- No crowded text walls
- No random images
- No low-resolution visuals
- No generic stock business photos

## Slide Design Ratio

Use:

- 60% whitespace
- 25% content
- 10% visual structure
- 5% cultural accent

# 14. CIM Writing Style

The CIM should feel like an institutional investment document, not a sales brochure.

Rules:

- Use complete sentences.
- Use clear section headings.
- Explain assumptions.
- Separate facts from projections.
- Avoid exaggerated claims.
- Include risks honestly.
- Use charts and tables for financial clarity.
- Keep cultural framing tied to governance and execution.

CIM tone:

> BGI is not asking investors to believe in an idea without structure. The strategy is grounded in lower-middle-market acquisition fundamentals: fragmented sectors, founder transition, operational inefficiency, disciplined entry structures, and post-close value creation.

- Move detail to appendix.
- Use one idea per slide.

## Problem: Too much hype.

Fix:

- Replace adjectives with proof.
- Add assumptions.
- Add risks.
- Add structure.

## Problem: Investor ask is unclear.

Fix:

- Add capital amount.
- Add use of funds.
- Add next step.
- Add investor role.
